Right turn: Austria’s presidency

No far-right candidate has been elected head of state in post-war Europe. But Norbert Hofer, a smooth-talking nationalist from the Freedom Party, is the favourite to beat the former Green Party leader, Alexander Van der Bellen, in Sunday’s run-off. This follows a political earthquake in the first round, with the elimination of candidates from the two centrist parties which have dominated Austrian politics for decades. The presidency is usually ceremonial, but Mr Hofer could make life difficult for the new social-democrat chancellor, Christian Kern. Mr Hofer has vowed to fire the government if it raises taxes or relaxes the new cap on refugees. Last year’s migrant crisis (mishandled by Mr Kern’s flip-flopping predecessor, Werner Faymann) stoked the popularity of the Freedom Party, which could become the largest party in parliamentary elections due in 2018. The election of an avowed xenophobe, even one with presentable manners, will send shivers across the continent.
